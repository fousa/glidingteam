The first Belgian participation in the World Gliding Championships was in 1952 in Madrid. Henry Gildemyn became 19th in the open class against 39 other pilots. Soon the team was expanded by pilots from both parts of the country for the next World Championships. Cartigny, Witter, Doutreloux, Baeke ... are just some names that represented our country in international championships. These pioneers paved the way for later generations of competition pilots.

Much has changed since then, but still the same basic rule applies: He who makes the fewest mistakes wins the game. The table below, with all top-10 places at European and World Championships by our compatriots, shows that Belgian pilots have put down extremely good performances.
