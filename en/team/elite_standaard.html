According to family tradition I had to fly before I could walk...

And so it happened, on the lap of my grandfather Albert en with my father (Al)Bert piloting the aircraft. Obviously, these two are the ones that thaught me all they know. My grandfather took responsibility for my training and agreed to let me fly solo at the age of 14. After obtaining my license my father became my tutor. He prepared me for and took me to my first cross-countries and he introduced me to the world of competitive flying.

Today my brother, Tijl, is my ideal training partner. Together we competed in several (junior) WGC's, we conquerred the Matterhorn in the sky, we tried our first 1000km flights in Namibia, we analyse flights and theories, ... all of this improves our skills a lot and provides additional motivation. Beside several victories at small and big competitions and 4 top-10-rankings at EGC/WGC's, our 6th and 8th place at the European Championships of 2009 are the highlight so far.

Because of the fact that almost all the male members of my family are gliding, I very early came in touch with the sport.

At age 15 I started taking flying lessons and when I was 16 I was allowed to try it for the first time by myself.
Thiswentwellandthatsameyear Iachieved my 5 hour duration flight, and the 50km and 300km cross country badges. The year after I flew my first 500km. My biggest flight in Belgium so far, was a 760km (OLC km) from the Keiheuvel in the LS8 of my father.

In terms of competitions I mostly participated in regional competitions, with mixed success.
I also flew four times the Belgian Championship and two World Championships, 1 in the junior team and 1 in the elite teams.
