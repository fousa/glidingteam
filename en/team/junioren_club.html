Name Jochen Schoeters
Date of birth 24/05/1991
Club Koninklijke Aeroclub Keiheuvel / LSV Viersen
Experience 550 h
Cross country 21.500 km
Glider Standard Cirrus
Best results 3rd place National Junior Competition 2009 / Venlo (NL)
  3rd place Zomerwedstrijden 2010 / Malden (NL)
  35th place German Junior Championships 2010 / Musbach (GER)

I'm flying through life as Jochen Schoeters, born on May 24, 1991 in Geel. Now I live in Balen and I study for the second year business management at the KHKempenin Geel.
Through my parents (who are glder pilots themselves) I spent my childhood at the airfield. In the year I turned 14, I began my training in the German gliding club "LSV Viersen", which flies at Keiheuvel. On an training camp in Stendal, near Berlin, I flew my first solo. Thereafter I had to fly close to the airfield, about 70 hours, before I was allowed togo cross country at the age of 16. I accomplished my first 50 km flight in the summer of 2007 departing from Stendal, in a Ka-8.

As a 17 year old, I flew my first 300 km, one year later followed by the 500 km flight. That was also the year where I would fly in my first competition. At the National Junior championships in Venlo (NL) I became third in the end. The nice part was that by the specific setup of this competition, I did learn a lot about competition flying.

In 2010 I took part in the"Zomerwedstrijden" in Malden (NL). Here again I got a third place. Afterwards, I would fly for the first time in the hilly countryside, surrounding Musbach in the Black Forest, during the German Junior National Championships. This was quite an event with many outlandings, often bad weather and finally a 35th place, but all in all I look back with satisfaction at this interesting experience.

In recent years I have always flown with the LS-4's or the Pegase of Kon.ACK and LSV Viersen. In late June - early July 2010, immediately after the exams, I had a great week where I fly six days in a row  and more than 3000 km from my startpoint, Keiheuvel. This winter we have a lot of work to do because we are refurbishin a Standard Cirrus which I can use to participate in the 2011 junior world championships in Musbach.

Name Rik Vanden Boer
Date of birth 22/03/1988

Experience 500 h
Cross country 5.500 km
Glider Standard Cirrus 75 WL "GX"
Best results 5th place clubclass Keiheuvelcup 2008 / Keiheuvel (BE)

  7th place clubclass BC 2010 / St.Hubert 2010 (BE)


The passion for gliding has come from father to son. At a young age I was already walking around at the airport in Hasselt to admire our great sport. At the age of 14 I started my gliding careerat "Albatros" on a Ka7. At the age of 16, I flew my first solo and then it went fast: Ka8, first cross-countries, 5 hours, 1000m altitude gain and 50 km. At the same time, I also applied for the air cadets. I have flown all the gliders in their fleet. During my first Kiewitcup, competing with a Jeans Astir in standard class, we where presented with a task of 360km. Without much hope, Simon Berx and I (both at Astir Jeans) started together on this taskt. The discharge was great when we returned at 19.30pm on Kiewit after a successful flight.

After my secondary school I continued my flying career, as a Bachelor in Aviation - Aspiring Pilot. This training and related practical flight training, have suppressed my gliding career a little bit.
Currently I work at JetairFly as a pilot in training and next year a new challenge might come up, a B737 type rating.

This winter we decided to buy a second glider within the family. Besides the Standard Libelle of my brother, Stijn, we found a Standard Cirrus 75 WL,formerly owned by the Rebbeck family in England.

Flying is still a passion for me. Each flight is a great and instructive experience.
