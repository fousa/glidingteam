  • High-tech up in the air

Just like in car racing or sailing, there are several categories of competition classes, depending on the performance of the aircraft. Each class has its own strengths and weaknesses. Thus, successively the most affordable is the club class, standard class is the most classical, the 15m racing class is one most focused on speed, the 18m class is the most competitive, the double-seater class is the newest and finally the open class is the most unrestricted one.

Gliders which participate in top competitions are well taken care of by their owners to get Â maximum possible performance out of them. In the small cockpits both ergonomic comfort and high-tech equipment - including GPS systems, loggers, final glide computers, variometers ... - ensure that the achieved speeds are unmatched by any other "clean" air sports.

Most of the production of gliders these days is based in the country where the sport originated, Germany. The three major manufacturers - Schempp-Hirth Flugzeugbau, Alexander Schleicher Flugzeugbau and DG Flugzeugbau - have gotten competition in recent years competition from Lithuania, Poland and South Africa.
Through extensive collaboration with universities who have wind tunnels at their disposal, aerodynamic perfection is approached. In particular, Prof. Loek Boermans from Delft (NL) is an authority in the field of wing profiles. But it's not only the performance of the gliders which are developed. Also safety, especially those of the cockpits, is similar to that of a Formula 1 racing car by using the strongest carbon fibres.
