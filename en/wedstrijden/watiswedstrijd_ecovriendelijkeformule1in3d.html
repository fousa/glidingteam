Gliding is a fascinating interplay between the pilot, aircraft and nature. With natural hot rising air currents sailplanes can make itineraries for hundreds of kilometers, without the use of an engine and without noise or harmful emissions. They reach top speeds up to 300 kp/h and the average speed for an entire task during a competition ranges from 100 to 150 kp/h.

The layout of a gliding contest is simple: the race committee sets a trajectory and the participants fly that route in the shortest possible time. The competition director opens the start line once all the pilots are in the air. Each pilot decides when the time is ready to leave on track. The turning points that determine the route are recorded by using GPS coordinates. GPS loggers on board the gliders are used to verify whether a participant actually has correctly flown the imposed trajectory.
It sounds simple, but such competition takes besides flight technical ingenuity a strong morale, a good physical condition and tactical cunning insight.

An important aspect during a competition at high level, is the mental strength of the pilots. The accompanying crews always tries to provide an excellent, while relaxing atmosphere. In this way, the final result is the merit of both pilot and crewmembers.
